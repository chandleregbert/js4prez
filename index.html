Joseph Smith announced his presidential candidacy on Jan 29, 1844
His pamphlet, General Smith's Views of the Powers and Policy of the Government of the United States was published February 7, 1844.
Some of his views reflected experiences he had suffered with the Saints:
"Give...the president full power to send an army to suppress mobs..."

"...no mob in Missouri expelling her citizens by executive authority..."
"...let Congress shew their wisdom by granting a national bank..."
This included his disappointment in Martin Van Buren:
"At the age, then, of sixty years our blooming Republic began to decline under the withering touch of Martin Van Buren! Disappointed ambition, thirst for power, pride, corruption, party spirit, faction, patronage; perquisites, fame, tangling alliances; priest-craft and spiritual wickedness in high places, struck hands, and reveled in midnight splendor."
He desired to eliminate the national debt:
"General Jackson's administration may be denominated the acme of American glory, liberty and prosperity, for the national debt, which in 1815, on account of the late war, was $125,000,000, and lessened gradually, was paid up in his golden day..."
national debt
He desired to shrink the size of the national government:
"Reduce Congress at least one half. Two Senators from a state and two members to a million of population, will do more business than the army that now occupy the halls of the National Legislature."
He also wanted to change their pay:
"Pay them two dollars and their board per diem; (except Sundays,) that is more than the farmer gets, and he lives honestly."
"Curtail the offices of government in pay, number, and power..."
He wanted to free every inmate:
"Petition your state legislatures to pardon every convict in their several penitentiaries; blessing them as they go, and saying to them in the name of the Lord, go thy way and sin no more."
He prefered service over incarceration:
"Advise your legislators when they make laws for larceny, burglary or any felony, to make the penalty applicable to work upon roads, public works, or any place where the culprit can be taught more wisdom and more virtue; and become more enlightened. Rigor and seclusion will never do as much to reform the propensities of man, as reason and friendship."
Except for murder:
"Murder only can claim confinement or death."
He had new plans for penitentiaries:
"Let the penitentiaries be turned into seminaries of learning, where intelligence, like the angels of heaven, would banish such fragments of barbarism: Imprisonment for debt is a meaner practice than the savage tolerates with all his ferocity."
He wanted to abolish slavery:
"Petition also, ye goodly inhabitants of the slave states, your legislators to abolish slavery by the year 1850, or now, and save the abolitionist from reproach and ruin, infamy and shame."
He had a plan to compensate slave owners:
"Pray congress to pay every man a reasonable price for his slaves out of the surplus revenue arising from the sale of public lands, and from the deduction of pay from the members of Congress."
HE BELIEVED:
  • "More economy in the national and state governments, would make less taxes among the people..."
  • "...more equality through the cities, towns and country, would make less distinction among the people..."
  • "...more honesty and familiarity in societies, would make less hypocrisy and flattery in all branches of the community..."
  • "...open, frank, candid, decorum to all men, in this boasted land of liberty, would beget esteem, confidence, union and love..."
He desired to have a National bank:
"For the accommodation of the people in every state and territory, let Congress shew their wisdom by granting a national bank, with branches in each state and territory..."
"...whose officers and directors shall be elected yearly by the people with wages at the rate of two dollars per day for services..."
"...banks shall never issue any more bills than the amount of capital stock in her vaults and the interest."
"The nett gain of the mother bank shall be applied to the national revenue, and that of the branches to the states and territories; revenues."
Other reforms Joseph Smith campaigned for:
  • ✔ Obtain Oregon
  • ✔ The nation should be from East to West coast
  • ✔ Allow Texas to join the US
  • ✗  Allow Canada and Mexico to join the US
  • ✔ Strengthen the navy
He shared his concern about the waning of American glory:
"No honest man can doubt for a moment, but the glory of American liberty, is on the wane; and, that calamity and confusion will sooner or later, destroy the peace of the people."
He wanted to emphasize the importance of honor:
"Abolish the practice in the army and navy of trying men by court martial for desertion; if a soldier or marine runs away, send him his wahes, with this instruction, that his country will never trust him again; he has forfeited his honor."
"Make HONOR the standard with all men: be sure that good is rendered for evil in all cases: and the whole nation, like a kingdom of kings and priests, will rise up in righteousness; and be respected as wise and worthy on earth; and as just and holy for heaven, by Jehovah the author of perfection."
He also placed emphasis on unity:
"Unity is power, and when I reflect on the importance of it to the stability of all governments, I am astounded at the silly moves of persons and parties, to foment discord in order to ride into power on the current of popular excitement..."
A presidential cry:
"We have had democratic presidents: whig presidents; a pseudo democratic whig president; and now it is time to have a president of the United States; and let the people of the whole union, like the inflexible Romans, whenever they find a promise made by a candidate, that is not practised as an officer, hurl the miserable sycophant from his exaltation, as God did Nebuchadnessar, to crop the grass of the field, with a beast's heart among the cattle."
"With the highest esteem,
I am a friend of virtue,
and of the people,
JOSEPH SMITH"
